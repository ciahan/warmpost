How it works

From your hands
to their windowsill

1

Draw an original card

Two ways to create — both equally welcome. Hand-drawn: use any paper and any medium — pencil, watercolor, marker, colored pencil. Digital: draw by hand on a tablet with a stylus using any drawing app. Either way, sign your first name and city on the card in your own handwriting. That signature is what makes it real.

2

Submit it here

Upload your card through our submission form. Tell us your name, where you're from, and what your card is about. We review every submission — only original, human-made artwork is accepted. No AI, ever.

3

We print and hand-deliver it

Your card is printed on card stock and personally delivered to an elder at one of our partner nursing homes in Maryland. We'll email you when it arrives so you know your card reached someone.

Why WarmPost exists

Every number in a dataset
is someone's story

"During my visits to nursing homes, I noticed some residents eagerly waiting for visitors while others spent long periods sitting quietly by themselves. I wondered — can something as simple as a handmade card remind someone that they are not forgotten?"

— Ciara Feng, founder of WarmPost and Generations in Tune

Who can submit

Anyone, anywhere
in the world

No art experience required — just a genuine card made by hand.

Hand-drawn artists

Pencil, marker, watercolor, colored pencil, pen — draw on any paper you have and photograph it in good lighting. No scanner needed.

Digital illustrators

Draw by hand on a tablet with a stylus — Procreate, Adobe Fresco, or any drawing app. Must be hand-drawn, never AI-generated.

International students

Submit from anywhere in the world. Tokyo, Dublin, São Paulo — your card will be printed and personally delivered in Maryland, USA.

Any age or grade

Middle school, high school, college, or beyond. No art experience required — just something genuine made by hand for a stranger who will treasure it.
